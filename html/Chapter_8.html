Level Design

Blockout

Now that you have planned your level it's time for a blockout. A blockout is a playable rough draft of the level. That means it is built with simple 3D blocky shapes.

Building this blockout gives you the opportunity to playtest your level before you put too much detail into it. You can change structures quickly and by testing it evaluate the flow, balance, encouter design and metrics and make changes according to your results.
The blockout is the design phase in which you finally discover if your ideas really work or not.

There are five commom 3D level construction methods in games:

  • Primitives: Simple shapes like cubes and boxes
  • Brushes/Modeling: Construction of 3D shapes in the level editor
  • Modular kit: Pre-made pieces are connected together
  • Sculpting: "Painting" organic 3D shapes (useful for landscapes)
  • Splines: Creating math curves that procedurally create geometry

    • Read more about theses methods here: The Level Design Book¹

    The basic process of the blockout is

    1. Sketch the layout
    2. Add the ground plane, scale figures, walls
    3. Playtest
    4. Diverge, iterate, playtest again

    You repeat step 4 until you're done.

    Things to look out for while testing:

  • Massing:

    Do the level's shapes make sense? Is it confusing to look at? Does the area feel big or small?

  • Metrics:

    Does the player fit through every door and passage? Do the stairs/ramps work? Can the player jump where they're supposed to jump?

  • Flow:

    Does the movement feel slow or fast, smooth or complex? What type of movement is ideal for your intended design goals?

    • Create a simple blockout for one of your levels.
