Mechanics

The mechanics are part of the skeleton of your game.

There are core mechanics which are the most apparent, most used and most important mechanics in the game.

There are also secondary mechanics that add to the core mechanics but aren't as important. Which ones these are totally depends on the game you're trying to make.

If you want to make a jump and run game your core mechanics would be jumping and running.

Check out Chapter 12 of The Art of Games Design by Jesse Schell to see how he categorizes mechanics.

Rules

One of the most fundamental mechanics in your game is rules. They define the course of action. What can the player do? What is he supposed to do?

Another important question is how these rules are enforced. Here are a few examples:

  • Fall damage:

    You don't want the player to be able to jump down from every hight

  • Restricting movement:

    You decide how high/far the character can jump, how fast they can run etc.

  • Restricting access:

    Don't want the player to enter a certeain building? Ensure the door wont open. Maybe they first need a key for it.

    • Think of more examples and define the rules for your game.

    Depending on the type of rule you might have to explain it to the player. If you're making a boardgame for example there is no way for the player to know how it works.
    Tutorials are created for that reason.
