Gametesting

When you want to make a game, gametesting is a crucial part of the process. This includes testing during the developement but also after.

During developement you are surronded by your game so much, that it can help to get other people's opinions. Some things might be crystal clear for you, but not understandable for other people, because they weren't part of the developement.

Testing the different parts of your game is important to safe time and money in the longterm. Imagine creating a level and being basically done and then you play it and realize it doesn't work out.

Here are some ways to test your game in different stages of developement:

  • If you are making a boardgame you might want to create a paper prototype and play it with some friends to find out if your rules make sense.
  • Try telling your story to a different person. Do they get it? When telling it, do you feel like something is missing?
  • The blockout is a big part of gametesting.
  • Early access: When your game is pretty much complete, but you want a feedback of a larger crowd it is possible to release an early access version of your game. This implies, that the game still has some kinks to it, but by having people play it you're going to find out what they are and you'll be able to fix them.

    • Jesse Schell gives a good explanation of playtesting and why it is so important The Art of Game Design - Chapter 27
